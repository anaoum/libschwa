If getting out of bed this morning was a struggle, tomorrow could be a real test, with the mercury set to fall to single digits.

According to the Bureau of Meteorology, Brisbane had a low of 10.3 degrees at 6.35am in the city.

But Weatherzone meteorologist Brett Dutschke said tomorrow morning was likely to be around the 9-degree mark.

"If 9 degrees does turn out to be the minimum, it's seven years since it's been this cold this early in the year," he said.

"And if it actually gets below 9 degrees, it's at least a decade since it would have done so this early in the year."

Mr Dutschke said Friday morning was likely to be colder again, with Brisbane's mild winter just weeks away.

"It's still pretty early in the month, and May's a transition month where it cools down quite a bit, but this weather system at the moment is very unusual for this time of year," he said.

"It's highly likely to be seven years since it's been this cold this early in the year, so it's quite a contrast since the recent nights.

"We had 16 degrees Sunday night/Monday morning, so it's a big change in only a few days."

A daytime maximum of 22 degrees is expected tomorrow, but Mr Dutschke said it would feel like the coldest day of the year so far.

"Tomorrow might feel like the coldest day this year, because it'll be the windiest day of the week," he said.

"Only a few days ago, the maximum was 18.9 and it's definitely going to be a low-20s day, but it'll feel colder because of that wind.

"There'll be plenty of sunshine, but that wind chill is going to be felt by a lot of people."

At this stage, the Bureau of Meteorology expects minimum temperatures to rise after Friday, with forecasts not dropping below double digits after tomorrow.

Maximums are expected to stay around the 22-23 degree mark for the next week.
